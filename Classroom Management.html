Classroom Management

Classroom Environment

I aim to create an environment where students feel creative and are encouraged to experiment and use their imaginations whilst being safe. In practical classes such as teaching students how to use equipment such as a scroll saw, and a drill press it is important to balance safety with engagement. Students will be encouraged to be creative in the classroom whilst adhering to guidelines that maintain their safety and the safety of everyone in the classroom.

Expectations and Mutual Respect

At the start of the year, I will collaboratively develop a classroom set of rules with the students. I will have a set of clear non-negotiable rules that need to be upheld in the classroom such as respecting myself and one another which ensures that the class will run smoothly, and people feel supported. These rules will be available to students and will be a contract between the students and myself, creating respect within the classroom.

Classroom Structure

In my different classrooms the rooms will be set up to be functional and efficient whilst still being collaborative learning environments. In rooms such as a textiles room the desks will be in a U-shape to encourage class discussion. In food technology rooms desks will be in groups of four to promote small group work within their allocated bays.
